# Claude rebuttal (proponent + concessions) — round 1.5

Codex's critique is strong and largely correct on the diagnosis. I concede the big
structural points up front, then defend the part of the avenue that survives, then
argue the disagreement is mostly about *framing and sequencing*, not about whether
channel-state-at-the-receiver is worth doing.

## Where Codex is right (concede)

1. **"DPD" is the wrong label.** Transmit-side pre-inversion of an unknown room is
   impossible here; what remains is receiver-side channel-state-information (CSI) +
   bit-loading, plus a small *universal* TX pre-emphasis. The RF-DPD analogy was a
   motivating frame, not a literal method. I drop the DPD framing. The honest name is
   **adaptive, CSI-driven receiver + capacity-oriented waveform/code**.

2. **The +6 is config-salvage, not a new frontier.** Verified: flat passes top out at
   333 gross bps; offline erasure adds only 4 passes at >=400 and 2 at >=467; 0/4 at
   480/533/640. So erasure-on-OOK does not, by itself, move the reliable-rate ceiling
   meaningfully above ~333-400 bps. Calling it "~1.9x bitrate" conflates yield with
   rate. Correct customer metric = "rate that passes nearly always across rooms,"
   not count of rescued configs. Concede fully.

3. **OOK + carrier-major + hard RS is near its own ceiling.** Erasure can only fix
   error mass that is a small set of identifiable carrier byte-bursts; it cannot fix
   residual hard-decision errors on non-erased carriers, and it throws away graded
   soft evidence. RS-erasure is close to the ceiling *of this architecture*. Concede.

4. **IMD/PAPR is a self-inflicted floor a receiver cannot remove.** The -10.5 dB
   off-grid floor is generated by all-ON high-PAPR multitone symbols. No amount of
   receiver CSI removes intermod that has already landed in an OOK "off" bin. This is
   the single most damning number for an *OOK* design and Codex is right to elevate it.

5. **OOK is the wrong primitive when the impairment corrupts "tone absence."** IMD,
   leakage, and room modes all put energy into off-bins — exactly where OOK reads a
   "0". DBPSK/coherent PSK keeps every carrier occupied and moves the decision off
   absolute amplitude. This is a real, structural argument for changing the waveform.

6. **The from-scratch adaptive_modem sync is broken, not merely unfinished.** It
   mis-measures 0.4% synthetic wow as 28% and erases nothing. That is a control-plane
   failure in the exact training path the proposal needs. Concede — and it means the
   *new-waveform* claim is unproven, only the *receiver-on-existing-data* claim is proven.

That is most of Codex's case. So what survives?

## What survives (the defensible proponent core)

**The disagreement is not "CSI receiver vs better waveform." It is sequencing and
shared infrastructure.** Codex's own preferred stack — soft LDPC with per-carrier
LLR scaling, bit-loading from pilot SNR, interleaving matched to the code — *is* the
adaptive CSI-driven receiver, just with a better code and waveform underneath. Every
one of Codex's higher-ROI items still requires the same live per-carrier reliability
estimate from a preamble:

- "scale LLRs per carrier" needs per-carrier SNR — that is the preamble CSI.
- "bit-loading from pilot SNR" needs per-carrier SNR — that is the preamble CSI.
- "erasure is just near-zero confidence in soft code" — concedes erasure was a
  *degenerate special case of the right thing*, not a wrong turn.

So the preamble + per-carrier SNR measurement is **not** thrown away by Codex's plan;
it is *promoted* from "drives erasure flags" to "drives LLR weights and bit allocation."
The avenue, correctly scoped, is: **measure the channel live at the RX from a tape
preamble, and let a soft-decision, bit-loaded, lower-PAPR waveform consume that CSI.**
That is the same avenue, with erasure-on-OOK demoted from "the method" to "the v0
proof that CSI helps." On that scoping, Codex and I largely *agree* and the fight is
about emphasis.

**Three places I push back on Codex:**

1. **Don't discard the proven, deployable, today-shippable erasure gain.** It is
   ~1.7x *yield* with zero re-record, zero extra parity, runs in the app on the
   *existing* tapes. For configs already near the 333-400 bps frontier it is free
   reliability. It also surfaced the RS-miscorrection -> mandatory-CRC bug, which any
   future soft-FEC design inherits. It is a floor to bank, not a hill to abandon.

2. **The IMD floor cuts toward TX-side waveform fixes too, which is the *cheapest*
   lever and Codex under-weights it relative to the FEC rewrite.** Lowering PAPR
   (Schroeder phases, avoid all-ON markers, drive control) is a *small, universal,
   tape-master* change — same on all 10 tapes, no room knowledge needed — and Codex's
   own arithmetic says a 3-6 dB IMD/SNR improvement moves ~two-thirds to ~nearly-all
   carriers into 2-bit territory. That is a bigger and cheaper ceiling move than a new
   LDPC stack. I think this is the highest-ROI single item and it should be tested
   *before* committing to a full soft-FEC waveform rewrite.

3. **Codex's kill experiment is the right experiment — and it largely vindicates the
   avenue rather than killing it.** A detector-domain Monte Carlo bakeoff from the real
   per-carrier on/off distributions, comparing hard-RS / RS-erasure / soft-FEC+LLR /
   bit-loading at 400/467/533/640 bps, costs only code. I accept it as the gate. But
   note its kill criterion ("RS-erasure >=95% at 467") is a test of the *OOK+RS*
   architecture, not of CSI-driven reception — and Codex *predicts soft-FEC+LLR wins*,
   which is the CSI receiver. So the experiment, on Codex's own prediction, concludes
   "keep channel estimation as LLR calibration; change the code/waveform." That is
   "continue the avenue, re-pointed," not "abandon it."

## Net position

- I concede: drop the DPD label; OOK+carrier-major+hard-RS is near ceiling; IMD/PAPR
  is the dominant self-inflicted impairment; erasure is salvage not a new frontier;
  the new modem's sync is genuinely broken; soft-decision FEC + interleaving +
  lower-PAPR + DPSK is the higher-ceiling direction.
- I defend: the *receiver-side live CSI from a preamble* is the through-line of BOTH
  plans and is not a dead end; the shipped erasure gain is a free floor; and the
  cheapest single lever is **PAPR/IMD reduction on the master**, which Codex's own
  numbers support, not the full FEC rewrite first.

The crux for round 2: **Is the cheapest path to higher reliable bitrate (a) lower-PAPR
waveform + bit-loading on the existing OOK/RS detector, or (b) a soft-decision FEC +
DPSK rewrite — and does the detector-domain bakeoff actually separate these, or does
it need a real re-recording to measure the PAPR/IMD change (which a detector-domain
replay of the OLD recording cannot, because it bakes in the old IMD)?** That last point
is a genuine methodological gap in the proposed kill experiment.
