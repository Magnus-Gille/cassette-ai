== THE AVENUE UNDER DEBATE ==
The team got far worse over-air bitrate than over a direct cable, and applied a DPD (Digital Pre-Distortion, from RF power-amplifier design) framing: use a known training sequence to MODEL the full acoustic channel and CORRECT for it. Because playback rooms are UNKNOWN and vary per use, transmit-side pre-distortion is mostly ruled out (can't pre-invert an unseen room); so the intelligence lives in the phone-app RECEIVER, ideally driven by a training preamble on each tape. The concrete proposal: DPD-inspired channel modeling + an adaptive (preamble-driven, bit-loaded, erasure / soft-decision) RECEIVER.

== MEASURED CHANNEL FACTS (verify against the files; do not take on faith) ==
- Over-air channel is GOOD on average: ~12.6 dB median per-carrier on/off SNR.
- But a sparse comb of deep frequency-selective NULLS: ~11% of carriers <8 dB, ~5.5% <5 dB.
- Clock offset stable ~0.89 (already resampled out, not the problem). Wow/flutter mild on the real recording (~6.5 ms marker jitter << 60-100 ms symbol).
- Nonlinearity / IMD floor ~ -10.5 dB (PAPR-driven, caps SNR).
- Production modem: K parallel OOK subcarriers 1500-7000 Hz, RS(48) FEC, pilot markers, CARRIER-MAJOR interleave. Result: 7/50 configs byte-exact. The 7 that pass are all low-rate (K20-24, 60-80ms, ~250-333 gross bps); every higher-K/faster config fails.
- Failure mechanism: each null becomes a per-carrier byte BURST; carrier-major interleave concentrates it; a few simultaneous bursts exhaust RS(48).

== ESTABLISHED RESULTS (real, non-circular) ==
- Receiver-side channel-aware ERASURE decode on the REAL recording: 7 -> 13 byte-exact (offline_proof.py, ~1.9x), no re-record, no extra parity. A deployable decision-directed variant: 7 -> 12 (adaptive_decode.py, ~1.7x). It also caught an RS mis-correction -> CRC needed as acceptance test.
- Shannon on measured per-carrier SNR (9 dB coding gap) says ~1.8-1.9x net bitrate headroom (bit-loading / 2-bit on strong carriers vs 1-bit OOK).
