# Acoustic data-over-sound — research survey (techniques to adopt)

Survey of proven data-over-sound systems, mapped to our OFDM modem
(`scripts/acoustic_ofdm_modem.py`). Generated by a research agent; condensed here.
Full priority table at the bottom.

## Systems surveyed

- **ggwave** (Gerganov): 16-ary M-FSK, 6 slots × 16 bins, F0=1875 Hz, dF=46.875 Hz,
  ~106 ms symbols, Reed-Solomon, **spectral-whitening XOR mask** before RS,
  **relative-amplitude marker detection** (no noise-floor needed), 8–16 B/s. The FSK
  "pick the loudest bin" idea is fundamentally more robust than our OOK threshold.
- **quiet / libquiet** (liquid-DSP): OFDM + cyclic prefix, GMSK, PSK/DPSK/QAM, Viterbi/RS.
  Good reference/testbed for DPSK-vs-OOK benchmarking.
- **chirp.io / AudioQR**: M-FSK + guard intervals + raised-cosine symbol shaping.
- **Google/G-Pay ultrasonic**: 63-ary MFSK + 127-chip DSSS PN (≈21 dB processing gain).
  PN sequence as a **preamble correlator** is the takeaway.
- **minimodem / Bell 202 / AFSK-1200**: 2-FSK 1200/2200 Hz, 1200 bps, cross-correlation
  demod (gives phase → enables DPSK).
- **Acoustic OFDM (MDPI 2025)**: cyclic prefix turns a frequency-selective channel into
  flat-fading subchannels; CP must exceed delay spread (~10–30 ms here, not full RT60).

## Cassette ancestors (directly relevant — this *is* the project)

- **Kansas City Standard (1976)**: 2-FSK, 0 = 4 cycles @1200 Hz, 1 = 8 cycles @2400 Hz,
  300 baud, **zero-crossing detection** (amplitude-immune). Our codec's 1200/2400 Hz tones
  are literally KCS frequencies.
- **ZX Spectrum**: pulse-width / edge-triggered, ~1364 baud, **5 s pilot tone** to settle
  motor + AGC before sync.
- **C64 Datasette**: pulse-length, ~50 B/s, block written **twice** (crude FEC), and —
  key — **measures pulse period during a 10 s leader to correct for motor-speed variance**
  (exactly our clock-drift problem, solved in 1977).
- **DeciLoad**: 8b/10b DC-balanced encoding for AC-coupled tape paths.

## Top findings mapped to our two failure modes

Our ~3–4% BER in noise is mostly: (1) **OOK amplitude threshold wrong** (iPhone AGC +
clock drift distort the all-on calibration), and (2) **bursty noise hits many
symbols×carriers at once**. The two highest-payoff fixes target these directly:

- **DBPSK instead of OOK** kills failure mode (1) — no threshold at all. Our Goertzel
  already computes the complex output; we currently throw away the phase. Multiply current
  symbol by conj(previous) → sign of real part = bit. ~1-line demod change in principle.
- **2D (time×freq) block interleaver** kills failure mode (2): write RS codewords into
  rows of a D×N matrix, transmit column-major; D=8 covers our lawnmower burst length.

## Priority-ranked actions for next session

| # | Technique | Payoff | Effort | Note |
|---|-----------|--------|--------|------|
| 1 | **DBPSK per carrier** (drop OOK) | very high | low | Goertzel phase already computed; eliminates threshold |
| 2 | **Barker-13 / MLS preamble + correlation** | high | med | sample-exact start + direct clock-ratio measurement |
| 3 | **Continuous pilot subcarriers** (2 of K) | high | med | track per-symbol phase rotation → correct Continuity drift |
| 4 | **2D rectangular block interleaver** | high | low | ~20 lines; handles burst + fade together |
| 5 | **Cyclic prefix (20–30 ms)** | high | high | unlocks coherent BPSK/QAM; structural change |
| 6 | **DSS whitening XOR mask** (from ggwave) | med | very low | free BER gain, prevents correlated tones |
| 7 | **Raised-cosine symbol taper** | med | low | less inter-carrier splatter |
| 8 | **Clock-ratio from preamble** (Datasette) | med | low | measure, resample, then demod |
| 9 | **DQPSK (2 bits/carrier)** | med | med | only after DBPSK stable; needs ~3 dB more SNR |

**Predicted:** DBPSK + 2D interleaver alone should drop BER to <0.5% — and remove the
iPhone-clock ceiling — likely pushing well past today's 467 bps gross.
